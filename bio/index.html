FINDERS KEEPERS

2022

In the hopes of giving their daughter a better chance at a good education bea is sent to live with her estranged grandmother at the beginning of middle school.

finders keepers is a feature length film I wrote to practice storyboarding scenes. The film deals with topics such as the correlation between poverty and poor education, familial estrangement and reconciliation, gender identity issues, learning to become your own person, self-care, and more.

MOVIE NIGHT

2020

this piece was inspired by a commission done as a virtual flyer for a party that included using a flashing effect in a .gif to simulate lightening.

it was done using photoshop on an ipad in a 3 hour period from start to finish.

i think this piece shows some of my technical ability in character design, color layout, and scene lighting. the linework is clean and the story is easy to read. proof of this is in the fact that the character’s emotions are clear, they’re posed dynamically, each object drawn is important to the narrative, the colors chosen are bold yet natural, and the lighting informs the mood.

TATE'S END?

2019

a 4" x 6" plexiglass etching with oil based inks printed on BFK rives. signed and edition AP.

sharon tate is famous for films such as valley of the dolls and don’t make waves, but might be better known for the way she was murdered by members on the manson family in 1969. this etching reflects on the aesthetics of the end title card of early hollywood cinema. It poses the question of whether sharon tate really met her end that day, or if she may still live on in her films.

MUSIC MUSES VOL.1

2023

music muses is a self-published zine containing 12 digital illustrations of various characters enjoying music. each character has a coresponding playlist with a track list that are related to the aesthetic portrayed in the character’s clothes and emotions.

this project was a practice in taking moods from music and portraying them as characters with stories that could be told in one image. each character is unique using color, clothing, props, and posing to give a sense of what kind of music they could be listening to.

the zine will sell at quimby’s from march-may 2023 and in my online shop until further notice.

THE SCREAM

2020

gouache and India ink on hot press bristol with projected squiggle hands. 18” x 24”

haphephobia is a fear of being touched. The severity of the phobia depends on the person. this piece aims to portray my inner feelings during the period of my life in which I attempted to overcome my own haphephobia after moving to a big city where personal space didn’t seem to exist. The distortion of the hands is to remind the viewer that while trigged someone might not be perceiving the world the same as everyone else.

A WILDNESS IN
THE GRID

2021

found materials, yarn scraps, paper, fabric scraps, crochet. the weaving is approximately 24” x 30” (not including the yarn tails that hang from the bottom).

A frame weaving, like all other forms of weaving, depends on the use of the grid to create images and shapes as you stack yarn horizontally. This piece was an attack on that grid by creating my own grid inside the weaving space and proceeding to hide it in the dredge of miscellaneous yarn trimmings used to create a muddy volume of color. The grid, now hidden from the viewer, (while technically impressive) is overlooked and ignored in favor of the wildness that sits atop it.

SIERR/SIERRA/STARS:
MY THREE FATES

2019

photographic manipulation using adobe photoshop and lightroom. all photographs belong to myself or sierra trostrud and the model is sierra trostrud.
(images used with the permission of the owner).

many grecian myths include the moirai, also known as the three fates. these are three sisters who control time and death. they’re often portrayed as hidden from the world malevolently balancing humanity’s mortality and this piece depicts them tempting the viewer into their hidden territory, what lies beyond their door? what can be learned from looking more intimately at a character that is so broadly stereotyped?

LIFE SIZE NUDES IN GRAPHITE

2022

two life size nude drawings in graphite on hot press paper roll displayed at the school of the art institute of chicago. approx. 2’ x 7’ not including extra rolled paper

each model sat for a period of 1.5 hours for a total of roughly 3 hours spent on this project. The ghostly treatment of the materials asks you to fill in spaces in the drawing. Strategically omitting sections of the figures bodies and the scene force the time spent observing each piece to be slowed down as the brain works. Our familiarity with the human form make it so that less is needed to read the figure as a figure, so just how much of a figure is necessary for us to recognize humanity?
